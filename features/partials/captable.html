Simple permissioning

Your large investors want to see your full cap table, and others just want to track their personal holdings. Easily define what each person can see, along with their latest cap table activity.

Supported by evidence

Most cap tables are just spreadsheets of numbers, but that leaves costly work for your lawyer and accountant to validate the data. Connect supporting documents to your transactions for a more organized cap table.

  • See the impact of your option pool Review the fully diluted percentage of your pool, vesting progress for grantees, and the number of options youve granted over time.
  • Term sheets can be confusing It helps to know what some of the terms mean, so we include helpful tips throughout to make sense of things.
  • Avoid versioning nightmares The days of creating versions for different shareholders are gone. Enter your data once and share everything, some things, or nothing at all.
  • Take your data with you Love spreadsheets too much to leave them completely? No problem. You can export all of the data from your cap table in a flexible CSV file.
  • Invite your lawyer & accountant Your cap table data isnt just for you and your shareholders. Share read-only views for things like auditing, fundraising, and tax reporting.
  • Multiple security types We keep your data conveniently structured by its security type. Keep track of your equity, debt, options, warrants and SAFEs.
